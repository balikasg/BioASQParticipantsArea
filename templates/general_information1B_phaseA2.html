Task 1B-Phase A Guidelines

* Guidelines for Task 1B will be further elaborated with information about evaluation measures, results submission and Python snippets. *
For a comprehensive description of the BioASQ tasks please visit bioasq.org.
Phase A of Task 1B will begin on Monday 17th of June, 2013. Three test sets of about 100 questions each will be released. A new test set will be available every three weeks. Participants, after downloading the test set will have to respond in 24 hours with the annotations for the questions of the test set that their systems estimated.

Training Data

The training data for both phases of Task 1B can be downloaded from here. The training data consists of 29 annotated questions in English. The questions have been created from biomedical experts around Europe. Four types of questions are available:
  1. Yes/no questions (decisive)
  2. Factoid questions
  3. List questions
  4. Summary questions
The annotations for each question include lists of:
  • concepts from designated ontologies and terminologies
  • relevant articles to the question from MEDLINE
  • snippets from the articles
  • RDF triples
Finally, an exact and an ideal answer that will be used in Phase B of Task 1B is also included for every type of question apart from summary questions. In summary questions only ideal answers exist. The training data are served as a JSON string with the following format: 
"questions":[
{"type":"factoid", 
"body":"Is Rheumatoid Arthritis more common in men or women?", 
"id":"5118dd1305c10fae750000010", 
"ideal_answer":"Disease patterns in RA vary between the sexes; the condition is more commonly seen in women, 
who exhibit a more aggressive disease and a poorer long-term outcome.", 
"exact_answer":"Women.", 
"documents":["http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12723987",..], 
"snippets":[{"document": "http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22853635",
	     "text": "The expression and clinical course of RA are influenced by gender. 
In developed countries the prevalence of RA is 0,5 to 1.0%, with a male:female ratio of 1:3.",
       	     "beginIndex": 559,
	     "endIndex": 718,
	     "fieldName": "sections.0"},..]
"concepts":["http://www.disease-ontology.org/api/metadata/DOID:7148",...],
"statements":[{ "s": "http://linkedlifedata.com/resource/umls/id/C2827401",
		"p": "http://www.w3.org/2008/05/skos-xl#prefLabel",
		"o": "http://linkedlifedata.com/resource/umls/label/A17680439"},...]
			}]
where:
  • type, is the type of the question
  • body, is the body of the question
  • ideal answer, is the ideal answer of the question
  • exact answer, is the exact answer of the question, except for summary questions that don't have an exact answer
  • documents, is a list of relevant documents, identified by their URIs in MEDLINE
  • concepts, is a list of relevant concepts, identified by their URIs
  • snippets, is a list of relevant snippets, identified by the URIs of the documents in MEDLINE they belong to, the offsets of the begin and the end of the snippet and the section where the snippets exists in the JSON. In the training set the actual snippet is also provided in the list, although it will not be requested in participants' results submission.
  • statements, is a list of statements, identified by the subject-predicate-object URIs
ATTENTION:
  • Only registered users can download the training set.
  • Users can use unlimited resources to train their systems.

Ontologies

The ontology sets that are available for Task 1B are:
  • the Medical Subject Headings (MeSH)
  • the Gene Ontology (GO)
  • the The Universal Protein Resource (UniProt)
  • the Joint Chemical Dictionary (Jochem)
  • the Disease Ontology (DO)
The above mentioned sets of ontologies are available for downloading in Submitting/Task 1B-Phase A.

Data Triples

The annotations of this phase of the task requires the usage of biomedical data expressed as triples, e.g., subject- predicate-object structured facts, extracted from biomedical resources or bibliography. The Linked Life Data project provides the LinkedLifeData platform. LinkedLifeData is a data warehouse that syndicates large volumes of heterogeneous biomedical knowledge in a common data model. The platform uses an extension of the RDF model that is able to track the provenance of each individual fact in the repository and thus update the information. It contains currently more than 8 billion statements, with almost 2 billion entities involved. The statements are extracted from 26 biomedical resources, such as PubMed, UMLS, DrugBank, Diseasome, and Gene Ontology.

Systems

Each user will have the opportunity to participate in Task 1B with a maximum of 5 systems. To enable this, after logging in you have to go to "Edit Profile Settings" and then click on the "Add System" button. The system name you will fill in the form will be the identifier of your system and it will be used in the "Results" section. ATTENTION: Trying to upload results without selecting a system will result in error while the results will not be saved.

Cluster

A large computer cluster has been made available to BioASQ. Users can run their code to the cluster for both Tasks of BioASQ Challenge.
